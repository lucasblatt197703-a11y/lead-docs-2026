The Craft

Photography taken to its natural extreme.

Isaac Azulay produces long-exposure photographs using exposures of 2 to 3 hours — a single frame, never composited. Where most photographers chase the decisive moment, he chases time itself: letting light accumulate, letting motion blur into geometry, letting cities dissolve into pure luminance.

Using neutral density filters and meticulous technique, each photograph is a record of hours compressed into one image — architecture of light that cannot be manufactured any other way.

One photograph. Up to 3 hours of exposure time. Zero post-processing composites.

How It Works

The long exposure process.

01
Location Scout

Hours of pre-visualization. Every long exposure begins with studying how light will move through a space over time — not just how it looks at a single moment.

02
ND Filter Setup

Neutral density filters reduce incoming light to allow shutter speeds of minutes — or hours. The camera sees what the human eye cannot: the totality of motion over time.

03
Single Frame

No composite. No blending. One shutter, one frame. The resulting image contains hours of light — traffic patterns, cloud movement, water, sky — compressed into a single exposure.

About

Born in Caracas.
Working in light.

Isaac Azulay is a photographer and educator based in Miami, Florida. Born in Caracas, Venezuela, he holds a degree in Communications from Reichmann University IDC (Israel) and an MBA from Nova University. Since 2017, he has dedicated his practice to mastering long exposure photography — a discipline that demands patience, precision, and a fundamentally different relationship with time.

"In my work I allow every tiny detail of my surroundings to be a valuable aspect — which may then become an enhanced image, an essential part of my art."
