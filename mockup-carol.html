The Artist

Carol McArdle

Carol McArdle moved to Southwest Florida in 1995 and has since become one of the state’s most recognized voices in wildlife and nature painting. Working in oil on canvas, she captures the birds, waterscapes, and vanishing landscapes of Florida with a realism that makes you look twice — roseate spoonbills, great egrets, mangrove shadows at golden hour. She has been named a finalist in the prestigious Birds in Art exhibit at the Woodson Art Museum twice, and her originals have sold for up to $19,000.
