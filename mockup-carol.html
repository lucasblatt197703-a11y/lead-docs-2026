02 Regal Roseate Oil on canvas · 48 × 36 inches

Among Carol's most recognized works, this portrait of a roseate spoonbill in full breeding plumage captures the electric pink that draws thousands of birders to Southwest Florida each winter. The layered impasto technique renders each feather with tactile immediacy — a still image that feels like it might breathe.

About

Working in oil and acrylic across canvases up to 60 inches, Carol McArdle has spent three decades painting the ecosystems of Southwest Florida — roseate spoonbills in the mangroves, sea grapes along the coast, flamingos in the last wild light. A two-time finalist in the Birds in Art Exhibition at the Woodson Art Museum, her work is held in private collections across the United States.

"I want to capture the beauty of the fast disappearing natural Florida."
