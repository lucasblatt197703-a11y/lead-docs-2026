The Process

01
Foundation

Each piece begins on pine wood or canvas — primed and sanded until the surface holds texture like wet sand holds a footprint.

02
First Pour

Acrylic and oil pigments are applied in layers — building tidal movement, beach gradients, and the soft violence of a breaking wave.

03
Resin Layers

Epoxy resin is mixed, tinted, and poured in controlled waves. Each layer cures for 24 hours. Some pieces take five or six pours to reach depth.

04
Glass Finish

A final high-gloss seal. Under light, the surface moves. Under shade, it holds still. Either way, it looks like the ocean from above.

The Artist
"Through the fusion of her Lithuanian heritage and the vivid influence of Florida, Zydra creates one-of-a-kind artworks that evoke the wonders of the sea."

Lithuanian-born and self-taught, Zydra Delaney is a full-time artist living and working in Merritt Island, Florida. Growing up beside the Baltic Sea, water has always been her native language. She moved to Florida's Space Coast and found its light, its color, and its coast waiting to be translated into paint and resin.

Her work is collected in the United States and Europe
