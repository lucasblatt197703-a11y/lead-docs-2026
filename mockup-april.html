The Craft

Step 01
Hand-cut & Formed

Each piece begins as raw copper sheet, hand-cut and formed into shape using traditional metalworking techniques passed down and refined over 9+ years of making.

Step 02
Textured & Patinated

Hammering, chasing, and chemical patination give each piece its distinctive depth — the warm burnished copper and sea-green verdigris tones that define the Copper Ridge Hill look.

Step 03
Finished by Family

Shawn, April, and the Raba kids complete every piece together — sealing, detailing, and preparing each work for display. No two pieces are ever identical.

The Artists

Shawn & April Raba
— Copper Ridge Hill

Working in copper, Shawn and April Raba have spent over nine years turning the textures of the sea, the warmth of the Florida coast, and the rhythms of coastal life into handcrafted pieces that collectors carry home from shows across the country.

Based in Ridgeway, SC, Copper Ridge Hill is a family operation — the Raba kids help design, shape, and sell alongside their parents at every show. The work is inseparable from the people who make it.

"For the Rabas, Copper Ridge Hill is more than a business — it's a way of life rooted in creativity, adventure, and togetherness."
