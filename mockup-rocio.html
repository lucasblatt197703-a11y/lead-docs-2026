Rocio MT's work is inseparable from her formation as an architect. Years of training in spatial abstraction, structural thinking, and environmental design inform every mark. The landscape is not backdrop — it is language.

Through her Miami-based studio, Tropico Design Studio, she continues practicing landscape architecture alongside her fine art — a dual practice that keeps each discipline vital, each feeding the other.

About the Artist

Dominican-American.
Miami-based.
Architecturally formed.

"My illustrations are the fruit of years of architectural training that emphasizes abstraction, juxtaposed with my natural tendency for figurative drawing."

Rocio MT is a Miami-based Dominican-American visual artist whose professional background in Architecture and Landscape Architecture shapes every aspect of her practice. Trained in visual art under the Dominican master Guillo Perez and later self-directed, her work synthesizes architectural precision with deep cultural and psychological exploration.

Her mixed-media work has been exhibited in the UNPHU 2017 Graduate Exhibition and published in the Calendar of Dominican Illustrations 2016 and the UNPHU Calendar 2018. She has been commissioned for illustrations for video games, portraits, corporate logos, and public murals.

Influenced by the psychedelic spiritual work of Alex Grey, the folk tradition of Haitian art, the figurative power of Dominican artist Nadal Walcot, and the landscape philosophy of Roberto Burle Marx, Rocio's practice bridges the designed world and the imagined one.
