Conservation Series

Sea Turtle
Paintings

Jan completed a dedicated series of sea turtle paintings to raise awareness of these endangered species, supporting sea turtle rescue, rehabilitation, and conservation efforts along Florida's Indian River Lagoon coastline.

The series spans multiple species — Green, Hawksbill, Flatback, and Loggerhead — rendered in her signature luminous watercolor technique.

Teach & Learn

Watercolor
Workshops

Jan teaches watercolor and acrylic workshops at locations across the country — from Cape Ann, Massachusetts to the National Parks of the American West. She teaches painting from life on location, helping artists at every level develop a looser, more expressive approach to light, atmosphere, and color.

Author & Illustrator

Children's Books &
Beacons of Light Studio

Mentored by Eloise Wilken, the legendary children's book illustrator for Golden Books/Western Publishing and Random House, Jan became an accomplished author-illustrator in her own right. Her imprint, Beacons of Light Studio, publishes books of inspirational verses that blend her fine art with her storytelling voice.

Jan's background spans film and television production, creative consulting for themed entertainment, and hosting travel series on CBS, PBS, and The Travel Channel — all informing the visual storytelling in her art.

The Artist

Jan Shaw King

Multiple award-winning American Impressionist based in Sebastian, Florida. Working primarily in watercolor and acrylic, Jan's paintings explore Western landscapes, coastal waters, and the urgent beauty of endangered species. Her loose impressionistic style — known for luminous skies, atmospheric perspective, and fluid brushwork — captures the story behind the moment.

Studied under master watercolorists Martin R. Ahern AWS, Zoltan Szabo, Ferdinand R. Petrie, and Carole A. Battle. Jan has shown in galleries and juried exhibitions across the country and entered her work for consideration in the American Watercolor Society's National Exhibition.
