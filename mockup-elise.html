About the Artist

Elise Ippolito

"I fell in love with painting faces at twelve years old — that fascination has never left."

Working primarily in oil and soft pastel, Elise Ippolito creates luminous portraits and landscapes inspired by the classical tradition of Mary Cassatt, Edgar Degas, and John Singer Sargent. After studying abroad in Paris, London, and Rome and practicing law in Tampa, she returned fully to her first love in 2005. Her work has been featured on CBS and NBC Tampa Bay, exhibited at the University of Tampa's Scarfone Gallery, the Progress Energy Art Gallery, and Bank of America Plaza's Artist Series.

Elise is also a committed philanthropist, donating time and artwork to the Junior League of Tampa and raising funds for the American Cancer Society and Children's Museum of Tampa.
