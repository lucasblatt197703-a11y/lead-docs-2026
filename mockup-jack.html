About

"Thirty-five years of restoring other people's work taught me what makes paint endure."

Casey's practice spans oil painting, sculpture, photography, and conservation — a body of work built over 35 years in Tampa, FL. His figurative oil portraits, painted from photographs with technical precision, are held in permanent collections at the Orlando Museum of Art, the City of Tampa, Hillsborough County, Alachua County, and the University of South Florida.

From 1978 to 1984, Casey led the restoration of the historic Tampa Theatre alongside a team of artists and sculptors. His abstract work has been his primary mode of expression since 1985, evolving through sculpture, public art commissions, and portraiture. He works from his Seminole Heights studio and continues to teach oil painting.
